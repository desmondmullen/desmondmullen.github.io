Paul McCartney - Off the Ground

Technical Director at Industrial Light and Magic for the official music video for the title song on Paul McCartney's album "Off the Ground". I had this opportunity to join ILM because everyone else there was tied up working on the original Jurassic Park. Together with Visual Effects Supervisor Scott Squires and Special Effects Artist Steve Gawley we used software Scott developed to composite video on a small farm of Macintoshes the same way that the rest of the crew at ILM was doing with film (much higher resolution) on a very large farm of SGIs. Proving quicker turnaround with our software over the SGI farm, my team produced tests for Jurassic Park Visual Effects Supervisor Dennis Muren of the banner fall in the now-famous T-Rex vs. Raptors fight scene.

WebTV Networks

I followed my mentor and friend, Bruce Leak, from Rocket Science to WebTV and was an early employee of this successful Silicon Valley startup, later acquired by Microsoft for half a billion dollars (an enormous sum at the time). Colleagues included Apple's QuickTime* team (led by Leak), one of the two creators of Apple's innovative cooperative multitasking Finder (originally called MultiFinder), Andy Rubin (later the driving force behind Android), and other influential Silicon Valley veterans. My duties at WebTV mainly included sole management of a large, demanding, and massively-expanding IT physical infrastructure, and on-site setup for our CEO's demos to CEOs of Intel and Netscape, and at large conferences like E3.

Magic Tree House

As Show Producer at the world-famous Morehead Planetarium in Chapel Hill, NC, I had the good fortune to work with Nobel Laureates, Apollo and Shuttle Astronauts, great authors and famous actors. While we produced a number of influential shows during my tenure there, Magic Tree House: Space Mission was the most successful. I was the Show Producer, 3D modeller and animator, musical director, and a voiceover talent for the show. Working closely with Will Osborne and Mary Pope Osborne and the rest of our team, we created the only multimedia representation of the phenominally successful Magic Tree House series of books. Due to popular demand, Magic Tree House was reworked for sale and distribution worldwide. Magic Tree House: Space Mission has gone on to be a popular show in major planetaria throughout the world.

The Busy Little Engine

I created this now-popular award-winning children's DVD because I had young children and wanted to do a science education show for young children with Morehead Planetarium. Preschool children were not part of the target audience for Morehead so it didn't make sense to do it that way. I decided to write, film, model, animate, produce and publish it myself and have been very gratified with the response.

Winner of the Telly Award for Excellence in Children's Programming! "...learning through imagination and curiosity in this fun DVD." Booklist Magazine, November 15, 2006
